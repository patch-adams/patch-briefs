The Lizard Brain Problem

When the amygdala senses threat, it hijacks the prefrontal cortex. Creativity shuts down. Risk-taking stops. We freeze.

In organizations built on fear of failure, this happens constantly. People stop iterating. Stop experimenting. Stop playing.

Carrie's work sits at this intersection: creating the psychological safety that lets the prefrontal cortex do its job.

Play can be really serious. Serious play. When you're in this zone where you're not worried about making mistakes, you're more likely to push boundaries.
